Publications

  • 2025.11.05
    Machine learning-accelerated screening of hydroquinone analogs for proton-coupled electron transfer
    ChemRxiv
    We used the AIMNet2 neural network to rapidly predict thermochemical properties (BDFE values) for ~200,000 hydroquinone-like molecules, revealing how backbone and N-substitution systematically tune PCET reactivity and validating the model with experimental data.
  • 2025.10.17
    Oxidation of C-H and O-H Bonds by a Copper Complex Inspired by the Cu(II)-Tyrosyl Species Formed in LPMO
    Inorganic Chemistry
    We report the first example of Cu complex supported by a redox-active ligand capable of oxidizing C–H bonds via proton-coupled electron transfer, revealing mechanistic parallels to Cu-dependent metalloenzymes and highlighting the role of non-thermodynamic factors in PCET reactivity.
  • 2025.05.30
    Synthesis and Characterization of Copper Complexes Featuring a Redox-Active ONO Ligand in Three Molecular Oxidation States
    Inorganic Chemistry
    We synthesized and characterized a family of Cu complexes with a redox-active ONO ligand that undergo reversible ligand-centered redox transformations across three oxidation states, providing evidence of how Cu–radical systems might tune their electronic structure to control reactivity.
  • 2024.12.16
    Enhanced Proton-Coupled Electron-Transfer Reactivity by a Mononuclear Nickel(II) Hydroxide Radical Complex
    Inorganic Chemistry
    We developed a NiOH complex supported by a redox-active ligand that accesses three oxidation states and promotes PCET reactivity, revealing that unique stereoelectronic factors such as radical character and basicity may enhance H-atom abstraction kinetics.
  • 2022.12.09
    C–H functionalization reactions promoted by 1st row transition metals with redox-active ligands. Nature did it first
    Handbook of CH-Functionalization, Wiley-VCH GmbH
    We review key examples of C–H functionalization catalyzed by 3d metal complexes that harness redox-active ligands, oxidants, and substrates relevant to the chemistry of natural metalloenzymes.
  • 2022.09.23
    Electroactive Polymer-Based Spray Ionization for Direct Mass Spectrometric Analysis
    Journal of the American Society for Mass Spectrometry
    We developed an electroactive polymer–based spray ionization method that enables rapid, miniaturized mass spectrometry with sample volumes as low as 0.1 μL, achieving up to 100-fold improved detection limits over paper spray for pharmaceuticals and peptides.
  • 2022.09.09
    A 4H+/4e– Electron-Coupled-Proton Buffer Based on a Mononuclear Cu Complex
    Journal of the American Chemical Society
    We developed a Cu-based 4H⁺/4e⁻ electron–coupled proton buffer that mediates both decoupled and coupled redox reactions—including catalytic O₂ reduction to water—by cycling between ligand-centered oxidation states via fast disproportionation.
  • 2021.10.16
    Enabling Conducting Polymer Applications: Methods for Achieving High Molecular Weight in Chemical Oxidative Polymerization in Alkyl- and Ether-Substituted Thiophenes
    Materials
    We showed that optimizing FeCl₃-mediated polymerization conditions by tailoring solvent and reagent addition significantly improves molecular weight in ether-substituted polythiophenes.
